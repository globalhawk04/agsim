# Rumen Fermentation Simulator Suite

**Interactive web-based tools for teaching and exploring rumen microbiology, thermodynamics, and beef cattle nutrition.**

Two complementary single-file HTML simulators:

- **Rumen H₂ Competition Simulator** — Deep dive into thermodynamic competition for hydrogen between microbial pathways.
- **Rumen Fermentation Simulator** — Full in-vivo sequence showing how diet and feed additives affect pH, VFAs, energy partitioning, methane loss, and animal performance.

Built for animal science education, extension, and research visualization.

## Features

### 1. Rumen H₂ Competition Simulator (`rumen-h2-simulator.html`)
- Real-time thermodynamic calculations (based on Thauer et al. 1977)
- Adjustable H₂ partial pressure and water activity
- Visual "interaction web" with animated particles flowing through pathways
- Special **Co-Feed Mode** showing mutual detoxification of nitrate + sulfate (van Zijderveld et al. 2010)
- Bonus **Quantum View** mode with virtual photons and tunneling
- Detailed enzyme steps, toxicity risks, and clinical notes

### 2. Rumen Fermentation Simulator (`rumen-fermentation-sim.html`)
- Adjustable beef finishing ration (starch, roughage, protein)
- Toggleable feed additives: Monensin, Nitrate, Sulfate, 3-NOP (Bovaer®)
- Animated "inside the cow" narrative with particles, lasers, and VFX
- Real-time rumen pH with SARA risk visualization
- Energy partitioning (NEm / NEg), A:P ratio, methane yield (Ym)
- Nitrogen synchrony and microbial protein synthesis
- Holographic final metabolic summary trees

Both tools run **entirely in the browser** — no installation, no server required.

## Quick Start

1. Download the two HTML files:
   - `rumen-h2-competition.html`
   - `rumen-fermentation-simulator.html`
2. Open either file in any modern web browser (Chrome/Edge/Firefox recommended).
3. Adjust sliders/toggles and explore!

## Science Behind the Tools

- Thermodynamics grounded in **Thauer et al. (1977)**
- Nitrate/sulfate detoxification based on **van Zijderveld et al. (2010)**
- Energy calculations inspired by **NRC (2016)** and Van Soest principles
- Methane mitigation mechanisms (3-NOP, ionophores, alternative H₂ sinks)

Perfect for teaching **interspecies hydrogen transfer**, VFA production, rumen acidosis, and sustainable methane reduction strategies.
